Understanding the Document

Sanction Letter


  • The Sanction Letter (SL) is prepared by the bank on its Letter Head specifying the Financial Facilities sanctioned to the CLIENT. It contains the type of financial facilities and limits both Funded as well as Non-Funded sanctioned to the Borrower.
  • Facilities like Inventory Funding, Sales Bills/ Purchase Bill Discounting backed by Letter of Credit, Inland Bills Discounting, Cash Credit, Overdraft , Term Loan, Working Capital Term Loan, Bank Guarantee, Letter of Credit, Working Capital Demand Loan, Forex Forward, Packing Credit, Dealer Funding etc, Rate of Interest, mode of repayment, penalties, processing fees, Security etc are clearly defined in a sanction letter.
  • The SL must be duly signed and accepted by the Authorised Signatory of the Borrower. All the pages of the SL requires full signature of the Authorised Signatory of the Borrower with rubber stamp- (wherever applicable) by the Borrower. The date of acceptance of the sanction letter to be filled in by the Borrower.
